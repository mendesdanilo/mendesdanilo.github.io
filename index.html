Noah and the Flood

By Jordan Peterson

There is no better pathway to self-realization and the annulment of being than to posit the highest good that you can conceive of and commit yourself to it. And then you might ask yourself, do you really have anything better to do ? And then if you don’t, why would you do anything else ? If you orient yourself properly and then pay attention to what you do everyday, that works. It is in accordance with what we understand about human perception because what happens is that the world shifts itself around your aim, you are a creature that has an aim, you have to have an aim in order to do something, and that sets up the world around you, it organizes all your perceptions, organizes what you see and what you don't see, it organizes your emotions and your motivations. So you organize yourself around that aim and then the day manifests itself as a set of challenges and problems and if you solve them properly then you stay on the pathway towards that aim and you can concentrate on the day. So you can have your cake and eat it too. You can point to the far distance but you can live in the day.
